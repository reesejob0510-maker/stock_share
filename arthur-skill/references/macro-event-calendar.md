# Macro Event Calendar for Crypto

Use this file to track scheduled macro events that affect the USD liquidity environment and crypto markets.

---

## Priority 1: Federal Reserve Events

### FOMC Meetings (8 per year, ~6 weeks apart)

The most important scheduled event for crypto liquidity.

**What to track**:
- Rate decision: hike, hold, or cut (and magnitude).
- Balance sheet guidance: QT pace adjustment, pause, or QE signal.
- "Dot plot" (Summary of Economic Projections, SEP): updated quarterly; shows rate path expectations.
- Fed Chair press conference language: forward guidance nuances.

**Typical crypto reaction pattern**:
- Hawkish surprise (hike > expected, hawkish language): BTC -5% to -15% within 24–48 hours.
- Dovish surprise (cut > expected or clear pivot signal): BTC +10% to +30% within days.
- In-line with expectations: minimal sustained move; positioning determines direction.

**Key dates**: FOMC dates are published 1 year in advance at federalreserve.gov/monetarypolicy/fomccalendars.htm.

**Pre-FOMC positioning**: In the ~2 weeks before FOMC, funding rates often compress (traders reduce leverage ahead of uncertainty). This can create a "buy the rumor" setup after positioning is cleaned.

## Priority 2: US Treasury Issuance

### Quarterly Refunding Announcement (QRA)

Treasury announces its debt issuance plans for the coming quarter. Large issuances drain private sector liquidity.

**Timing**: Early in each quarter (first Wednesday of February, May, August, November).

**What to track**:
- Total issuance amount: higher than expected = more liquidity drain.
- Duration mix: more long-duration issuance = more pressure on bond yields.
- Buyback programs: Treasury buying back existing bonds = liquidity injection.

**Crypto relevance**: A surprise QRA with higher-than-expected issuance can trigger a risk-off move. A lower-than-expected issuance is mildly bullish for risk assets.

### Debt Ceiling Dynamics

**Critical pattern** (review this at each debt ceiling episode):

1. **Ceiling approached**: Treasury uses "extraordinary measures" to extend runway.
2. **X-date** (when Treasury exhausts extraordinary measures): Debt ceiling must be raised or defaulted.
3. **TGA drawdown phase**: While ceiling is binding, Treasury cannot issue new debt → spends down TGA → liquidity injection into private sector.
4. **Resolution**: Congress raises or suspends ceiling.
5. **TGA refill phase**: Treasury issues large amounts of bills/notes to rebuild TGA → short-term liquidity drain.
6. **Normalization**: Returns to regular spending and issuance.

**Crypto timing**: The TGA drawdown phase (step 3) is the bullish window for crypto. The TGA refill (step 5) is the headwind.

## Priority 3: US Inflation Data

### CPI (Consumer Price Index) — Monthly

**Release**: Monthly, ~10th of the following month. Bureau of Labor Statistics (bls.gov).

**What to track**:
- Headline vs Core (ex-food and energy).
- Month-over-month vs year-over-year.
- Super core (services ex-housing): the Fed's preferred measure.
- Shelter component: lagging indicator but largest component.

**Crypto relevance**: Surprise hot CPI = Fed tightening fears = risk-off = BTC pressure. Surprise cool CPI = Fed easing hopes = risk-on = BTC positive.

### PCE (Personal Consumption Expenditures) — Monthly

The Fed's *preferred* inflation gauge. More impactful than CPI for Fed policy decisions.

**Release**: Monthly, last Friday of the following month. Bureau of Economic Analysis (bea.gov).

## Priority 4: Labor Market Data

### Non-Farm Payrolls (NFP) — Monthly

**Release**: First Friday of each month. Bureau of Labor Statistics.

**What to track**:
- Total jobs added vs consensus.
- Unemployment rate.
- Wage growth (Average Hourly Earnings): wage inflation feeds into PCE.

**Crypto relevance**: Strong NFP → Fed can maintain tightening → risk-off. Weak NFP → Fed may ease → risk-on. However, in a regime where the Fed is explicitly data-dependent, each print matters more.

## Priority 5: Regulatory Calendar

### SEC Crypto-Specific Events

- Bitcoin ETF: approval/denial decisions; application deadlines.
- Enforcement actions against exchanges, token issuers: announced without scheduled warning.
- Congressional testimony: Fed Chair, SEC Chair appearances with crypto questions.

### International Regulatory Events

- **EU/MiCA**: Markets in Crypto-Assets regulation implementation timeline.
- **Hong Kong**: Crypto exchange licensing; retail crypto trading rules.
- **Japan FSA**: Exchange registration; stablecoin regulations.
- **India**: Crypto taxation and regulatory status announcements.
- **China**: Periodic reiteration or change in crypto stance (historically negative).

## Priority 6: Banking System Stress Indicators

Hayes's specific observation: crypto historically benefits from perceived banking system stress, as BTC positions itself as "outside the system."

**Early warning indicators**:
- LIBOR-OIS spread (interbank lending stress): spike > 50bps = stress.
- KBW Bank Index (KBWB): sharp decline = banking stress.
- TED spread: Treasury bill rate vs Eurodollar rate; spike = dollar funding stress.
- Regional bank CDS (credit default swaps): rising = market pricing default risk.
- Fed emergency facilities activated (BTFP, discount window usage surge).
