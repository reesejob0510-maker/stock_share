# Risk and Research Boundary

Use this file for high-risk investment prompts.

The normal experience should stay conversational. Keep the boundary clear and light.

## Core boundary

The Skill provides research support:

- ranked research priorities by moat quality and valuation;
- evidence and uncertainty;
- moat and competitive advantage reasoning;
- financial quality and capital allocation assessment;
- risk and downside checks;
- next verification actions.

The user makes the trading decision.

Use a short line when needed:

`I will rank this by research priority. The trading decision is yours.`

Chinese:

`我会按优先研究价值排序。买卖动作由你自己决定。`

---

## High-risk situations

Use extra caution when:

- the company is micro-cap or thinly traded;
- the moat thesis is mainly circulating on social media without filing support;
- the idea depends on one unconfirmed large customer or contract;
- the company has new equity financing, convertibles, placements, heavy dilution, or unusual insider selling;
- the business has negative FCF while requiring significant capital to grow the moat;
- the valuation assumes sustained high growth with no margin of safety;
- the moat is built on a single patent or regulatory license nearing expiry;
- geopolitical exposure, sanctions, or export controls drive a key revenue stream.

---

## Allowed strong judgments

Strong views are useful when the evidence supports them:

- `I would prioritize this company first because its moat is widening and the valuation is below intrinsic value.`
- `This ranks higher because the ROE track record and FCF quality are the strongest in the sector.`
- `This is still only a lead — there is no filing evidence of pricing power yet.`
- `I would downgrade it if gross margin starts declining when the next annual report is published.`

Chinese:

- `我会把这家公司放第一优先级，护城河在加宽，估值在内在价值以下。`
- `它排得更高，是因为 ROE 记录和 FCF 质量在这个行业最强。`
- `这个现在还只是线索，还没有财报层面的定价权证据。`
- `如果下一份年报里毛利率开始下降，我会降低优先级。`

---

## Avoid

- guaranteed return language;
- direct buy/sell order instructions;
- coordinated buying or group-pump language;
- rumor-based recommendations;
- material non-public information;
- invented prices, filings, customers, contracts, or market caps;
- personal portfolio allocation as if the user's full financial context is known.

---

## Practical safety checks

Before ranking an individual security highly, check:

1. Latest filings and any recent equity financing or balance sheet change.
2. Moat source: is there recent evidence it is intact, or are there erosion signals?
3. FCF conversion and financial quality vs the thesis.
4. Capital allocation history: has management earned the benefit of the doubt?
5. Valuation: is the margin of safety real, or does it depend on heroic growth assumptions?
6. Governance and related-party risk (especially A-share and HK).
7. What one observable fact would make the thesis weaker?
