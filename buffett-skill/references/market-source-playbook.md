# Market Source Playbook

Use this file when market-specific evidence matters for moat and valuation research.

---

## US Markets

**Primary filings**
- SEC EDGAR: 10-K (annual), 10-Q (quarterly), DEF 14A (proxy), 8-K (current events), 13-F (institutional holdings including Berkshire).
- Earnings call transcripts: Seeking Alpha, Motley Fool, company IR pages.
- Berkshire Hathaway 13-F: quarterly filing showing Buffett's current public equity holdings (filed 45 days after quarter end).

**Berkshire Hathaway Annual Letters (1977–present)**
- Primary source for methodology. Letters available at berkshirehathaway.com.
- Key letters for framework: 1986 (owner earnings), 1987–1989 (moat concept), 2007 (capital allocation).

**Moat evidence sources**
- Consumer brand: Nielsen market share data, retail channel checks, pricing trend data.
- Network effects: platform DAU/MAU filings, customer growth transcripts.
- Switching costs: renewal rate disclosures, contract duration commentary in earnings calls.
- Cost advantage: gross margin comparison across 10-K peer disclosures.

**Valuation cross-checks**
- Historical multiple data: Macrotrends, Wisesheets, Bloomberg.
- FCF yield: calculated from 10-K cash flow statement + market cap.
- Insider activity: SEC Form 4 (real-time insider transactions).

## A-share (China Mainland)

**Primary filings**
- 年报 (annual), 半年报 (interim), 季报 (quarterly): SSE (Shanghai), SZSE (Shenzhen), BSE (Beijing).
- 临时公告: material event disclosures including contracts, agreements, investments.
- 交易所问询函: exchange inquiry letters — highly valuable for understanding auditor concerns.
- 互动易 (SSE) / 上证 e 互动 (SZSE): direct Q&A between management and retail investors.

**Moat evidence for A-shares**
- Pricing power: gross margin trend across 3–5 year 年报; compare against peers in same SIC code.
- Switching costs: 合同负债 (contract liabilities) as a proxy for prepayments / deferred revenue.
- Cost advantage: 单位成本 disclosures in 年报 management discussion; compare per-ton or per-unit economics.
- Capital allocation: 分红率 (payout ratio) and buyback announcements via 临时公告.

**Red flags (A-share specific)**
- 关联交易: related-party transactions — check if material and arm's-length.
- 商誉 (goodwill): rapid goodwill growth from acquisitions; check impairment risk.
- 股权质押: controlling shareholder pledging shares — signals financial stress.
- 定增 / 可转债: equity dilution signals; check if capital raised for growth or to cover cash burn.
- 应收账款增速 > 收入增速: receivables growing faster than revenue — collection quality concern.

**Useful search paths**
- cninfo.com.cn: full filing database (巨潮资讯).
- 同花顺 / Wind / 东方财富: financial data aggregators.
- 互动易: investor Q&A platform for SSE-listed companies.
- 招投标信息: government procurement data for B2G companies.

## Hong Kong Markets

**Primary filings**
- HKEX (hkexnews.hk): annual reports, interim reports, circulars, announcements.
- Earnings call transcripts: company IR pages, Bloomberg.
- Connected transactions: disclosed in circulars; important governance signal.

**Moat evidence for HK**
- Many HK-listed companies are China operating; apply A-share moat analysis.
- For Hang Seng large caps: use HKEX filings + Bloomberg for historical multiple data.
- Southbound flow (深港通/沪港通): China retail/institutional money flowing into HK — can indicate cross-border sentiment, not moat signal.

**Red flags (HK specific)**
- Placement history: frequent equity issuance at discount to market price = dilution risk.
- H/A premium or discount: understand which drives the other; A-share trading suspension can disconnect.
- Liquidity: average daily volume below $5M HKD makes position management difficult.

## Japan

**Primary sources**
- TDnet (tdnet.info): Tokyo Stock Exchange filings.
- Annual reports (有価証券報告書): detailed financial statements.
- Monthly operating data: for retail, auto, and real estate sectors — highly valuable for moat verification.

**Moat considerations**
- Japanese companies often have strong operational moats but poor capital allocation history (excessive cash hoarding, low ROE from over-capitalization).
- Governance reforms (2023–) improving buybacks and dividend policy — watch for moat companies improving capital returns.

## Europe

**Primary sources**
- Company IR pages: annual reports, sustainability reports.
- Euronext, Deutsche Börse, London Stock Exchange filings.
- Earnings transcripts: Bloomberg, company IR.

**Moat considerations**
- Strong brand moats in luxury (LVMH, Hermès), industrial machinery (German Mittelstand), and healthcare.
- Currency exposure: EUR/USD/GBP volatility can distort reported earnings vs owner earnings in base currency.
- ESG regulatory pressure: increasingly affects capital allocation for European industrials and energy.
