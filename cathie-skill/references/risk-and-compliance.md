# Risk and Research Boundary

Use this file for high-risk investment prompts.

## Core boundary

The Skill provides research support:

- S-curve position and technology adoption analysis;
- Wright's Law cost trajectory and threshold identification;
- TAM scenario ranges at convergence;
- company ranking by innovation layer position and evidence quality;
- risk and downside checks;
- next verification actions.

The user makes the trading decision.

Use a short line when needed:

`I will rank this by research priority. The trading decision is yours.`

Chinese:

`我会按优先研究价值排序。买卖动作由你自己决定。`

---

## High-risk situations

Use extra caution when:

- the company is pre-revenue or pre-product with a large market cap driven by narrative;
- the TAM estimate requires multiple uncertain assumptions stacking on each other;
- the cost curve projection depends on a single company's technology roadmap;
- the company needs significant capital raises before reaching FCF positive;
- the thesis depends on regulatory approval timing (FDA, NHTSA, NDRC) which is inherently uncertain;
- the technology has a credible leapfrog risk from an alternative platform;
- the company is in a government-subsidy-dependent market where policy can change;
- the S-curve position is disputed — some data shows early majority, other data shows still in chasm.

---

## Important disclaimer on ARK methodology

This skill is inspired by ARK's publicly available methodology. It does NOT:
- replicate ARK's internal models or proprietary data;
- use ARK's closed-source TAM calculations;
- reproduce ARK's specific 5-year price targets (these change frequently and require internal models).

ARK's Big Ideas reports are cited as medium-confidence evidence, not as ground truth. Verify ARK's TAM assumptions independently where possible.

---

## Allowed strong judgments

Strong views are useful when the evidence supports them:

- `I would prioritize this layer first because the cost threshold crossing is supported by independent benchmark data.`
- `This company ranks higher because it has the fastest learning rate in the sector, backed by BNEF and filing data.`
- `This is still only a lead — there is no independent cost benchmark confirming the company's production cost claims.`
- `I would downgrade it if the next BNEF battery price survey shows the learning rate compressing.`

Chinese:

- `我会把这个方向放第一优先级，因为独立基准数据支持成本拐点已经到来。`
- `它排得更高，是因为它的成本曲线学习率最快，有 BNEF 和财报数据支撑。`
- `这个现在还只是线索，没有独立数据确认公司的生产成本声明。`
- `如果下一次 BNEF 电池价格调查显示学习率压缩，我会降低优先级。`

---

## Avoid

- guaranteed return language;
- direct buy/sell order instructions;
- implying that technology timelines are certain ("this WILL happen in 2027");
- hype around pre-revenue innovation companies without flagging capital risk;
- using "disruption" as decoration — only use it when substitution evidence exists;
- invented cost data, TAM figures, penetration rates, or market caps;
- personal portfolio allocation as if the user's full financial context is known.

---

## Practical safety checks

Before ranking an innovation company highly, check:

1. Is there independent evidence (not just company guidance) of cost curve progress?
2. Does the company have enough capital to reach the next cost threshold?
3. Is there a credible alternative technology that could leapfrog this platform?
4. Does the government policy or subsidy environment support or create dependency risk?
5. Is the S-curve position evidence-based, or is it driven by investor narrative?
6. What one observable fact would make the thesis weaker?
