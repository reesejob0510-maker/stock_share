# Cross-Asset Allocation Framework

Use this file when translating a GLI phase judgment into specific cross-asset allocation implications.

---

## Core principle

In Raoul Pal's framework, the GLI phase is the primary driver of cross-asset allocation. The demographic/debt supercycle provides the long-term asymmetric bias toward hard assets. The narrative window determines which assets have the strongest near-term momentum.

---

## GLI Phase → Asset Class Matrix

### GLI Expansion (early phase)

**Macro context**: CBs cutting or signaling cuts; M2 accelerating; credit impulse turning positive.

| Asset | Raoul's bias | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| BTC / ETH | Strongly overweight | Highest beta to global liquidity; leads the expansion |
| Gold | Overweight | Real rate suppression; monetary debasement hedge |
| Equities (broad) | Overweight | Liquidity tailwind; earnings growth returning |
| Quality growth equities | Overweight | Long-duration assets benefit from falling real rates |
| EM equities | Overweight | USD typically weakening; EM benefits from risk-on |
| Government bonds (long) | Overweight | Rate cuts → bond price appreciation |
| USD | Underweight | Weakens as liquidity expands |
| Commodities | Moderate | Demand recovery; also benefits from USD weakness |

**Positioning guidance**: In early expansion, accumulate BTC aggressively. Raoul describes this phase as the "asymmetric window" — the cost of being wrong (missing the first 50% of a move) is higher than the cost of being right (participating in the full move).

---

### GLI Expansion (mid-to-late phase)

**Macro context**: Expansion underway; inflation may be rising; CB rhetoric beginning to turn cautious.

| Asset | Raoul's bias | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| BTC / ETH | Overweight but reduce leverage | Beta still high but crowd building |
| Altcoins | Moderate (last to run) | Speculative cycle; highest risk/reward |
| Gold | Overweight | Inflation hedge as prices rise |
| Equities (quality) | Hold | Earnings strong; but valuation risk rising |
| Commodities | Overweight | Demand peak; supply constraints |
| Government bonds | Reduce | Rising inflation erodes real value |
| USD | Neutral | Begins recovering as CB rhetoric shifts hawkish |

**Positioning guidance**: Still invested but reduce concentration in highest-beta, reduce leverage in crypto. Rotate from pure beta to some quality.

---

### GLI Peak / Plateauing

**Macro context**: Rate hikes beginning or signaled; M2 growth decelerating; credit impulse rolling over.

| Asset | Raoul's bias | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| BTC / ETH | Reduce significantly | Contraction approaching; high beta = high risk |
| Altcoins | Exit or minimal | Most vulnerable to contraction |
| Gold | Hold | Inflation still elevated; hedge remains valid |
| Quality equities | Underweight | Multiple compression as rates rise |
| Defensive equities (value, dividends) | Hold | Less rate-sensitive |
| Short-duration bonds | Accumulate | As rates peak, short bonds become attractive |
| USD | Overweight | Strengthens as tightening begins |

**Positioning guidance**: "The exit is not the last move up — it's the first sign that liquidity is turning." Reduce risk before the clear peak, not after.

---

### GLI Contraction

**Macro context**: Active tightening; M2 flat or declining; credit impulse negative; economic slowdown.

| Asset | Raoul's bias | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| BTC / crypto | Minimal or zero | Highest beta = worst performer; 70–85% drawdowns historical |
| Equities | Underweight | Multiple compression + earnings decline |
| Gold | Hold / slight overweight | Maintains inflation hedge; may dip initially as USD rallies |
| Long-duration government bonds | Accumulate (if inflation falling) | Rate cuts coming eventually; bonds benefit |
| USD (short-term) | Overweight | Flight to safety |
| Cash / short-term T-bills | Overweight | Capital preservation; real yield turns positive |

**The Raoul mental model**: During contraction, the goal is to NOT lose money (preserve purchasing power). The next expansion will offer better opportunities to compound.

---

### GLI Trough / Pre-Expansion

**Macro context**: Tightening ending; CBs signaling pivot; leading indicators turning; credit impulse bottoming.

| Asset | Raoul's bias | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| BTC | Aggressively accumulate | Historically leads expansion by 6–12 months |
| ETH | Accumulate | Also leads but slightly lagging BTC |
| Quality growth equities | Accumulate | Long-duration assets benefit first from rate peak |
| Gold | Accumulate | Real rate peak → gold re-rates |
| Long-duration bonds | Accumulate | Rate cuts will appreciate bond prices |
| USD | Reduce | Will weaken as expansion begins |

**The asymmetric entry**: Raoul's strongest conviction moment. If the GLI is at trough and about to turn, the risk/reward for BTC is the best it will be for years. The challenge: identifying the trough before it is confirmed.

## Bitcoin's Special Role in Raoul's Framework

Raoul distinguishes between two roles for BTC in a portfolio:

### BTC as tactical beta

- Used to amplify returns during GLI expansion.
- Position size varies with GLI phase.
- High beta = high allocation when liquidity expanding; minimal allocation when contracting.

### BTC as long-term structural position

- Based on the demographic/debt supercycle thesis.
- Permanent allocation (Raoul calls this "HODL" allocation) as a hedge against monetary debasement.
- Not adjusted based on GLI phase (long-term structural position).
- Sized as a % of total wealth that can be held through a 70–80% drawdown without affecting lifestyle.

**Practical application**: Separate the two. A structural BTC position is not tactical — it is a long-term savings vehicle. The tactical GLI-based overlay sits on top.

## ETH vs BTC Allocation

Raoul uses both but with different rationale:

| Attribute | BTC | ETH |
|---|---|---|
| Primary thesis | Digital gold / debasement hedge / liquidity beta | Programmable settlement layer / technology S-curve |
| GLI beta | Very high | Very high |
| Technology optionality | Low (store of value focus) | High (DeFi, L2, smart contracts) |
| Relative behavior | Leads market cycle | Follows BTC but more volatile |
| Recommended ratio | Primary (larger allocation) | Secondary (smaller, higher beta) |

In early expansion: BTC > ETH. In mid-cycle when technology narrative takes over: ETH may outperform.

## DXY (US Dollar Index) as Cross-Asset Signal

Raoul consistently monitors the DXY as a proxy for global risk appetite and liquidity conditions:

- **DXY rising**: Risk-off, dollar shortage, global tightening; BTC under pressure.
- **DXY falling**: Risk-on, dollar abundant, global easing; BTC benefits.

**Practical use**: If GLI signals mixed but DXY is clearly trending down, it confirms the liquidity is flowing into risk assets.

**Data source**: DXY on TradingView, Bloomberg, or any major financial data provider.
